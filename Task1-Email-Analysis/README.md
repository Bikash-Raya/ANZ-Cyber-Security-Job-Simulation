# Task 1: Social Engineering - Email Analysis

## 📧 Overview

In this task, I analyzed 7 emails to determine whether they were **safe** or **malicious**. This simulates the daily work of a cybersecurity analyst investigating potential phishing attempts.

## 🎯 Objective

- Review each email carefully
- Identify indicators of phishing or social engineering
- Classify each email as Safe or Malicious
- Provide detailed justification for each classification

## 🔍 Methodology

For each email, I analyzed:
1. **Sender Information** - Domain, display name consistency
2. **URLs/Links** - Legitimacy, typosquatting, redirects
3. **Content** - Grammar, urgency, generic language
4. **Attachments** - Suspicious file types
5. **Social Engineering Tactics** - Authority, fear, urgency
6. **Context** - Does the email make sense?

## 📊 Results Summary

| Email | Classification | Key Indicator |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Email 1 | ✅ Safe | Personal conversation between friends |
| Email 2 | ❌ Malicious | Russian domain (.ru) for Microsoft email |
| Email 3 | ❌ Malicious | Fake Facebook URL (typosquatting) |
| Email 4 | ✅ Safe | Legitimate forwarded marketing email |
| Email 5 | ❌ Malicious | Social engineering - account access request |
| Email 6 | ✅ Safe | Legitimate internal corporate communication |
| Email 7 | ❌ Malicious | Suspicious URL with mismatched subject |

**Detection Rate:** 7/7 correctly identified
